The Creation of Bitcoin

Bitcoin was introduced in 2008 by an anonymous person or group of people under the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. Nakamoto published the Bitcoin whitepaper, outlining the concept of a decentralized digital currency, free from central authority. The first transaction using Bitcoin occurred in 2009, and since then, Bitcoin has evolved into a leading digital asset.

Bitcoin's Early Adoption

Bitcoin gained significant attention in the early years, especially in 2010 when a programmer named Laszlo Hanyecz made the first-ever purchase using Bitcoin—ordering two pizzas for 10,000 BTC. This event demonstrated Bitcoin’s potential as a medium of exchange, even though it was initially used mostly by tech enthusiasts and cryptographers.

Mainstream Recognition

Bitcoin began to attract widespread attention by the mid-2010s. It became a household name and started being used in real-world transactions, including retail purchases and investments. In 2017, Bitcoin reached its peak price of nearly $20,000, solidifying its place as a key player in the financial world. Bitcoin has since been followed by thousands of other cryptocurrencies, all influenced by the original idea of decentralized digital money.
